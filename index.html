About me

I am Anand Jacob Abraham, an Assistant Professor in the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering at IIT Kharagpur. I completed my BTech in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Calicut, my MTech in Industrial Engineering and Management from the National Institute of Technology Calicut, and my PhD in Management Studies from the Indian Institute of Science Bangalore. My research focuses on resource economics, cooperative game theory, and pricing problems in supply chain management. In 2022, I received the Indian Economics Association medal in recognition of the academic contributions of my PhD work. My research has been published in prestigious journals such as Mathematical Social Sciences and Economics Letters.

Research

I am interested in topics of Resource economics, Game theory, Supply chain management, and Applied operations Research. Here is a brief overview of my research activities.

River sharing and pollution

This research studies how pollution affects water allocation decisions and cooperation in river sharing.

Associated publication:
Abraham, A., Ramachandran, P. (2019). Effect of Pollution on Transboundary River Water Trade. In GDN 2019. View

Bilateral river water markets and dams

This study explores how upstream dam construction affects water trade in shared rivers.

Associated publication:
Abraham A., Ramachandran P. (2021). The welfare implications of transboundary storage and dam ownership on river water trade. Mathematical Social Sciences, 109, 18–27. View

Flood cost sharing

We propose the SUPA rule to fairly allocate flood damages among riparian states using cooperative game theory.

Associated publication:
Abraham A., Ramachandran P. (2020). A solution for the flood cost sharing problem. Economics Letters, 189, Article 109030. View

Water sharing agreements in flood-prone environments

This work studies stability of water sharing contracts in stochastic flood-prone settings using dynamic game theory.

Associated publication:
Abraham A., Ramachandran P. (2021). Stable agreements with fixed payments on transboundary flood-prone rivers. View

Availability forecasting

This research compares machine learning models for streamflow forecasting on the Periyar river using EEMD.

Associated publication:
Maiti R., Menon B.G., Abraham A. (2024). Ensemble empirical mode decomposition based deep learning models for forecasting river flow. Expert Systems with Applications, 255. View

Dual channel supply chain price competition

This research analyzes how dual channel supply chains react to competition under different pricing strategies.

Associated publications:
Nair R.B., et al. (2022). Prices and profits in centralized dual-channel supply chains under competition. IFAC-PapersOnLine, 55(10). View
Nair R.B., et al. (2024). Optimal pricing decisions of centralized dual-channel supply chains in a duopoly. Sadhana, 49(1). View
